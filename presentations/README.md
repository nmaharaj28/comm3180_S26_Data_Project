## Presentations for COMM3180 Group Data Project

* This folder contains the subfolders and files for your project presentations.

### Initial Presentation

Demographics of E.G.O.T. Winners (Demographics_of__E_G_O_T__Winners_.pdf)
Our initial presentation, delivered early in the semester before the project's scope was narrowed to the Oscars. It covers the research questions motivating a cross-award demographic analysis of Emmy, Grammy, Oscar, and Tony winners; provides institutional background on each of the four awards (governance structure, category count, voting body, and historical scope); outlines the four primary data sources (Academy Award winners from data.world, Tony Award winners from r/Broadway, Grammy winners from Kaggle, and Emmy winners from Kaggle) along with their known gaps; and describes the planned analytical pipeline — per-award demographic frequency distributions, decade-by-decade diversity trend lines, cross-award chi-square comparisons, and an EGOT master-spreadsheet analysis tracking the ~28 competitive and honorary EGOT winners by win order, demographics, and nationality.

### Poster Session Presentation

Are the #OscarsSoWhite? (oscarssowhite_presentation.pdf)
Our poster session presentation, delivered mid-semester after the project was refocused on the #OscarsSoWhite campaign and its measurable effects. The 9-slide deck covers the project idea, research questions, and hypotheses; data sources (Wharton/Annenberg Twitter Archive, box office data from Kaggle, and Oscar nomination and demographics data merged and completed with Claude's assistance); nomination diversity analyses showing a statistically significant post-2016 step change in POC nomination rates, with all acting and directing nominees in 2015 being white; box office analyses showing POC-led nominees historically and currently outperforming white-led nominees at the median across every major category; audience analysis of a hand-coded sample of 100 #OscarsSoWhite tweets (2015–2022) broken into supportive, oppositional, and neutral stances, with most tweets classified as neither; an emergent story slide synthesizing the three threads; and a next-steps slide outlining LLM-assisted coding of the full tweet dataset, Google Trends analysis, and post-ceremony longevity tracking of the hashtag.
